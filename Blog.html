What are different types of exercise??

Exercise is defined as a type of movement that is intentional, structured, and habitual. There are four different types of exercise – aerobic, strength,and flexibility. The health benefits of exercise are broad and include improving mental, cardiovascular, and structural well-being. Research has also shown that regular exercise protects against many chronic conditions, excessive weight gain, and obesity. Engaging in regular exercise is arguably one of the best approaches to improve your health and increase longevity.

Aerobic:

By definition, aerobic means “requiring oxygen”. During aerobic exercise, the oxygen you inhale is carried to your muscles, providing them with the energy needed to perform.

1.Jump Rope


  1. Equipment: Gym shoes (sneakers), jump rope
  2. Benefits: Jumping rope helps develop better body awareness, hand-foot coordination, and agility
  3. Safety: Your jump rope should be adjusted for your height.
  4. Duration and frequency: 15 to 25 minutes, 3 to 5 times per week

2. Running or jogging

  1. Equipment: Gym shoes
  2. Benefits: Running is one of the most effective forms of aerobic exercise. It can improve heart health, burn fat and calories, and lift your mood, just to name a few.
  3. Safety: Choose well-lit, populated running routes. Let someone know where you’ll be.
  4. Duration and frequency: If you’re a beginner, run for 20 to 30 minutes twice a week. Your pace should be conversational during the run. You can alternate between 5 minutes of running and 1 minute of walking to start.

Strength:

Strength training, also commonly referred to as resistance or weight training.Strength training aims to build and maintain muscle tissue through the use of bodyweight or external resistance,

1.Deadlifts

  1. Equipment: Flat Converse Shoes, Lifting Belt, Weightlifting Chalk
  2. Benefits: Helps improve posture and stability, It engages more muscles, Easy to perform and affordable
  3. Safety: To avoid injury be sure to bend your knees about 30 degrees. Bend down slowly towards your feet.
  4. Duration and frequency: 2 to 5 times a week Weekly Structure 1 strength-focused day (moderate reps and moderate loading) 1 to 2 skill days (higher reps and lighter/moderate loading) 1 to 3 deadlift variation days

2.Planks

  1. Equipment: Flat Converse shoes
  2. Benefits: Apart from working on Abdominal Muscles it also affects back Muscles, Shoulders, Glutes, Arms, and Chest.
  3. Safety: Engage your core during the entire movement to prevent your back from getting strained or injured.
  4. Duration and frequency: Perform each exercise for 20–60 seconds. Rest for 30–60 seconds between exercises. Repeat up to 4 times. Bend down slowly towards your feet.

Flexibility:

Flexibility exercise is just another word for stretching. To put it simply, it’s the group of exercises in which muscles or tendons are deliberately flexed or stretched to create more elasticity. The more elasticity in our muscles and tendons, the easier it is to move around.

1. Standing Quad Stretch

  4. Duration and frequency: 2 to 5 times a week Weekly Structure 1 strength-focused day (moderate reps and moderate loading) 1 to 2 skill days (higher reps and lighter/moderate loading) 1 to 3 deadlift variation days
